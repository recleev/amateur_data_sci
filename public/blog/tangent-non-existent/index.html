1 Definition of a Tangent Line

Consider a curve \(y = f(x)\), and let \(P\) be a given fixed point on this curve. Let \(Q\) be a second nearby point on the curve, and draw the secant line \(PQ\). The tangent line at \(P\) can now be thought of as the limiting position of the variable secant as \(Q\) slides along the curve toward \(P\). — Simmons (2016) restating Fermat’s definition of a tangent line

2 Slope of a Tangent Line on a Curve

\[m = f'(x_0) = \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{f(x_0 + \Delta x) - f(x_0)}{\Delta x}\]

3 Condition for Existence

For a tangent line to exist, the secant line \(PQ\) must approach the same tangent line at \(P\) in both directions. For a limit to exist, it must approach the same value in both directions.

4 Limit of an Absolute Value

4.1 Problem

Find \(f'(x_0)\) when \(x_0 < h\), \(x_0 > h\), and \(x_0 = h\) for,

\[y = f(x) = \lvert x - h \rvert\]

4.2 Solution

\[ \begin{align} f'(x_0) &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{f(x_0 + \Delta x) - f(x_0)}{\Delta x} \\ \\ y &= f(x) = \lvert x - h \rvert \\ \\ f'(x_0) &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{\lvert x_0 + \Delta x - h \rvert - \lvert x_0 - h \rvert}{\Delta x} \end{align} \]

If \(x_0 < h\), then \(\lvert x_0 - h \rvert = h - x_0\)

\[ \begin{align} f'(x_0) &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{[h - (x_0 + \Delta x)] - (h - x_0)}{\Delta x} \\ \\ f'(x_0) &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{-\Delta x}{\Delta x} \\ &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0} -1 \\ &= -1 \end{align} \]

If \(x_0 > h\), then \(\lvert x_0 - h \rvert = x_0 - h\)

\[ \begin{align} f'(x_0) &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{[(x_0 + \Delta x) - h] - (x_0 - h)}{\Delta x} \\ \\ f'(x_0) &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta x} \\ &= \lim \limits_{\Delta x \to 0} 1 \\ &= 1 \end{align} \]

The limits approach different values as \(x_0 \to h\) from the left and from the right. This means that the tangent line at \(x_0 = h\) does not exist.
