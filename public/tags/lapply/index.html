Two One-Thousand Samples from a Million and the Birthday Problem

2019 Dec 20
ggplot2 / lapply

1 No Overlapping Samples 1.1 Solved in R 1.2 Combination 2 Birthday Problem 3 Session Info 1 No Overlapping Samples John D. Cook presented another nice probability problem: Suppose you draw 1,000 serial numbers at random from a set of 1,000,000. Then you make another random sample of 1,000. How likely is it that no numbers will be the same on both lists? Cook did provide a proof that the probability of two \(n\) samples from a set of size \(n^2\) not having similar members approaches \(1/e\) as \(n \to \infty\).
