1 Problems

1.1 Region in the Second Quadrant

Problem 17, page 255 in Simmons (2016)

Find the area of the region in the second quadrant bounded by the \(x\)-axis and the parabolas \(y = x^2\) and \(y = \sqrt{x + 18}\).

1.2 Cubic Curve and Tangent

Problem 18, page 255 in Simmons (2016)

Find the area between \(4y = x^3\) and its tangent at \(x = -2\).

2 Solutions

2.1 Horizontal Strip

When solving for areas bounded by regions, graphing the lines and shading the areas of the region of interest makes finding the equation to integrate easier.

Figure 2.1: Area in the second quadrant bounded by the \(x\)-axis and the line \(y = x^2\) and \(y = \sqrt{x + 18}\).

For R users, please refer to (ref:code-plot) for the plotâ€™s code.

I chose to use a horizontal plot because I think this is easier to use. The horizontal strip has an area \(dA\), thickness \(\Delta y\), and length defined by the difference of the abscissas of the two lines, i.e. \(x_2 - x_1\) where \(x_2 = - \sqrt{y}\) and \(x_1 = y^2 - 18\). Note that \(x_2 = \pm \sqrt{y}\), but since the given is in the second quadrant, then \(x_2 = - \sqrt{y}\). The area of the horizontal strip then is

\[ \begin{equation} dA = \left[- \sqrt{y} - \left(y^2 - 18\right) \right] dy \tag{2.1} \end{equation} \]

To find the area of the bounded region, we need to find the limits of integration with respect to \(y\). The minimum is 0, but the maximum will need a little algebra.

\[ \begin{align} y &= x^2 \\ y &= \sqrt{x + 18} \\ x^2 &= \sqrt{x + 18} \\ x^4 &= x + 18 \\ x^4 - x - 18 &= 0 \\ x^4 - x - 16 - 2 &= 0 \\ x^4 - 16 - x- 2 &= 0 \\ \left( x^2 - 4 \right) \left( x^2 + 4 \right) - \left(x + 2 \right) &= 0 \\ \left( x - 2 \right) \left( x + 2 \right) \left( x^2 + 4 \right) - \left(x + 2 \right) &= 0 \\ \left( x + 2 \right) \left[ \left( x - 2 \right) \left( x^2 + 4 \right) - 1 \right] &= 0 \\ x + 2 &= 0 \\ x &= -2 \\ y &= 4 \end{align} \]

The point \((-2, 4)\) is a point on the second quadrant and the only point we are interested in, so we will not bother with the other roots1. We now have the limits of integration2 to evaluate equation (2.1). The area, \(A\) of the bounded region is

\[ \begin{align} A &= \int_0^4 \left[- \sqrt{y} - \left(y^2 - 18\right) \right] dy \\ \\ &= \int_0^4 \left(- y^{ \frac{1}{2}} - y^2 + 18 \right) dy \\ \\ &= \left[ - \frac{2}{3} y^{\frac{3}{2}} - \frac{1}{3} y^3 + 18y \right]_0^4 \\ \\ &= - \frac{2}{3} \times 4^{\frac{3}{2}} - \frac{1}{3} \times 4^3 + 18 \times 4 \\ \\ A &= \frac{136}{3} \end{align} \]

3 Between the Lines

Before we can find the area, we must first find the equation of the line tangent to \(4y = x^3\) at \(x = -2\) or the point \(\left( -2, -2 \right)\). Since the line is a tangent, the slope of the line will be defined by \(\cfrac{dy}{dx}\) at \(x = -2\).

\[ \begin{align} 4y &= x^3 \\ \\ y &= \frac{x^3}{4} \\ \\ \frac{dy}{dx} &= \frac{3}{4} x^2 \end{align} \]

At \(x = -2\), the slope of the line is \(m = 3\). Since we know that the point passes through \(\left( -2, -2 \right)\)

\[ \begin{align} y &= mx + b \\ -2 &= 3 \times -2 + b \\ b &= 4 \\ y &= 3 x + 4 \tag{3.1} \end{align} \]

We can now plot the two lines to better understand the problem.

Figure 3.1: Area between \(4y = x^3\) and its tangent at \(x = -2\).

When I saw this plot, I started to wonder if we have to find the area from \(\infty\) to \(- \infty\). However, my review is yet to enter that topic3, so I will focus on the area between the intersections of the cubic function and the tangent line. I also took the liberty to use a vertical strip4.

\[ \begin{equation} dA = \left( 3x + 4 - \frac{x^3}{4} \right) \Delta x \tag{3.2} \end{equation} \]

We already know that the first intersection in at \(x = -2\), so we only need to find the upper bound.

\[ \begin{align} y &= \frac{x^3}{4} \\ \\ y &= 3x + 4 \\ \\ \frac{x^3}{4} &= 3x + 4 \\ \\ x^3 &= 12x + 16 \\ \\ x^3 - 12 x - 16 &= 0 \\ \\ x^3 - 4x - 8x - 16 &= 0 \\ \\ x \left( x^2 - 4 \right) - 8 \left( x + 2 \right) &= 0 \\ \\ x \left( x - 2 \right) \left( x + 2 \right) - 8 \left( x + 2 \right) &= 0 \\ \\ \left( x + 2 \right) \left[ x \left( x - 2 \right) - 8 \right] &= 0 \\ \\ \left( x + 2 \right) \left( x^2 - 2x - 8 \right) &= 0 \\ \\ \left( x + 2 \right) \left( x + 2 \right) \left( x - 4 \right) &= 0 \\ \\ x &= -2 \\ x &= 4 \end{align} \]

Observe that I used the knowledge that one of the roots is \(x = -2\).

We can proceed to find the area between \(y = \frac{x^3}{4}\) and \(y = 3x + 4\) using equation ??.

\[ \begin{align} A &= \int_{-2}^4 \left( 3x + 4 - \frac{x^3}{4} \right) dx \\ \\ A &= \left[ \frac{3}{2} x^2 + 4x - \frac{1}{16} x^4 \right]_{-2}^4 \\ \\ A &= \frac{3}{2} \left( 4 \right)^2 + 4 \left( 4 \right) - \frac{1}{16} \left( 4 \right)^4 - \left[ \frac{3}{2} \left( -2 \right)^2 + 4 \left( -2 \right) - \frac{1}{16} \left( -2 \right)^4 \right] \\ \\ A &= 27 \end{align} \]
