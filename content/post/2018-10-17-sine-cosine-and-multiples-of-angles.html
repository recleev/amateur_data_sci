1 Review of Trigonometric Identities

Here is an overview of some of the most important trigonometric identites. In my opinion, one can derive other trigonometric identities just by knowing these.

\[ \begin{align} \sin \left( - \theta \right) &= - \sin \theta \tag{1.1} \\ \\ \cos \left( - \theta \right) &= \cos \theta \tag{1.2} \\ \\ \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta &= 1 \tag{1.3} \\ \\ \sin \left( \theta + \phi \right) &= \sin \theta \sin \phi + \cos \theta \sin \phi \tag{1.4} \\ \\ \cos \left( \theta + \phi \right) &= \cos \theta \cos \phi - \sin \theta \sin \phi \tag{1.5} \end{align} \]

A special identity for equations (1.4) and (1.5) happens when \(\theta = \phi\).

\[ \begin{align} \sin \left( 2 \theta \right) &= 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \tag{1.6} \\ \\ \cos \left( 2 \theta \right) &= \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \tag{1.7} \end{align} \]

2 Problems

2.1 Quadruple Angle

Problem 62, page 50 in Simmons (2016).

Verify:

\[ \begin{align} \sin 4 \theta &= 8 \sin \theta \cos^3 \theta - 4 \sin \theta \cos \theta \tag{2.1} \\ \\ \cos 4 \theta &= 8 \cos^4 \theta - 8 \cos^2 \theta + 1 \tag{2.2} \end{align} \]

2.2 Multiple Angles Sine and Cosine Product

Problem 63, page 50 in Simmons (2016).

Prove:

\[ \begin{align} \sin m \theta \sin n \theta &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta - \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta \right] \tag{2.3} \\ \\ \sin m \theta \cos n \theta &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \sin \left( m + n \right) \theta - \sin \left( m - n \right) \theta \right] \tag{2.4} \\ \\ \cos m \theta \cos n \theta &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta + \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta \right] \tag{2.5} \end{align} \]

3 Solutions

3.1 Quadruple is Double Double

Problems (2.1) and (2.2) can be solved using identities (1.6), (1.7), and (1.3).

\[ \begin{align} \sin 4 \theta &= \sin 2 \left( 2 \theta \right) \\ &= 2 \sin 2 \theta \cos 2 \theta \\ &= 2 \left( 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \right) \left( \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \right) \\ &= 4 \sin \theta \cos \theta \left[ \cos^2 \theta - \left( 1 - \cos^2 \theta \right) \right] \\ &= 4 \sin \theta \cos^3 \theta - 4 \sin \theta \cos \theta + 4 \sin \theta \cos^3 \theta \\ \sin 4 \theta &= 8 \sin \theta \cos^3 \theta - 4 \sin \theta \cos \theta \\ \\ \cos 4 \theta &= \cos 2 \left( 2 \theta \right) \\ &= \cos^2 2 \theta - \sin^2 2 \theta \\ &= \cos^2 2 \theta - \left( 1 - \cos^2 2 \theta \right) \\ &= 2 \cos^2 2 \theta - 1 \\ &= 2 \left( \cos 2 \theta \right)^2 - 1 \\ &= 2 \left( \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \right)^2 - 1 \\ &= 2 (\cos^4 \theta - 2 cos^2 \theta \sin^2 \theta + \sin^4 \theta) - 1 \\ &= 2 \left( \cos^4 \theta - 2 cos^2 \theta \sin^2 \theta + \sin^4 \theta \right) - 1 \\ &= 2 \left[ \cos^4 \theta - 2 cos^2 \theta \left( 1 - \cos^2 \theta \right) + \left( 1 - \cos^2 \theta \right)^2 \right] - 1 \\ &= 2 \left( \cos^4 \theta - 2 cos^2 \theta + 2 \cos^4 \theta + 1 - 2 \cos^2 \theta + \cos^4 \theta \right) - 1 \\ &= 2 \left( 4 \cos^4 \theta - 4 cos^2 \theta + 1 \right) - 1 \\ &= 8 \cos^4 \theta - 8 cos^2 \theta + 2 - 1 \\ \cos 4 \theta &= 8 \cos^4 \theta - 8 cos^2 \theta + 1 \end{align} \]

3.2 Add and Subtract

Problems (2.3), (2.4), and (2.5) already offer clues on the proofsâ€™ process. The left hand side of the equations can be seen in identities (1.4) and (1.5). We will also need identities (1.1) and (1.2) to simplify the sine and cosine of the negative angles.

\[ \begin{align} \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta &= \cos \left( m \theta + n \theta \right) \\ \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta &= \cos m \theta \cos n \theta - \sin m \theta \sin n \theta \tag{3.1} \\ \\ \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta &= \cos \left( m \theta - n \theta \right) \\ &= \cos m \theta \cos \left( - n \theta \right) - \sin m \theta \sin \left( - n \theta \right) \\ \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta &= \cos m \theta \cos n \theta + \sin m \theta \sin n \theta \tag{3.2} \\ \\ \sin \left(m + n \right) \theta &= \sin \left( m \theta + n \theta \right) \\ \sin \left(m + n \right) \theta &= \sin m \theta \cos n \theta + \cos m \theta \sin n \theta \tag{3.3} \\ \\ \sin \left(m - n \right) \theta &= \sin \left( m \theta - n \theta \right) \\ &= \sin m \theta \cos \left( - n \theta \right) + \cos m \theta \sin \left( - n \theta \right) \\ \sin \left(m - n \right) \theta &= \sin m \theta \cos n \theta - \cos m \theta \sin n \theta \tag{3.4} \end{align} \]

We derive (2.3) by subtracting (3.2) from (3.1).

\[ \require{cancel} \begin{align} \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta - \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta &= \cos m \theta \cos n \theta - \sin m \theta \sin n \theta - \left( \cos m \theta \cos n \theta + \sin m \theta \sin n \theta \right) \\ &= \cancelto{0}{\cos m \theta \cos n \theta - \cos m \theta \cos n \theta} - \sin m \theta \sin n \theta- \sin m \theta \sin n \theta \\ \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta - \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta &= -2 sin m \theta \sin n \theta \\ \sin m \theta \sin n \theta &= - \frac{1}{2} \left[ \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta - \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta \right] \\ \sin m \theta \sin n \theta &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta - \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta \right] \end{align} \]

Problem (2.4) by adding (3.3) and (3.4).

\[ \begin{align} \sin \left(m + n \right) \theta + \sin \left(m - n \right) \theta &= \sin m \theta \cos n \theta + \cos m \theta \sin n \theta + \left( \sin m \theta \cos n \theta - \cos m \theta \sin n \theta \right) \\ &= \sin m \theta \cos n \theta + \sin m \theta \cos n \theta + \cancelto{0}{\cos m \theta \sin n \theta - \cos m \theta \sin n \theta} \\ \sin \left(m + n \right) \theta + \sin \left(m - n \right) \theta &= 2 \sin m \theta \cos n \theta \\ \sin m \theta \cos n \theta &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \sin \left(m + n \right) \theta + \sin \left(m - n \right) \theta \right] \end{align} \]

Finally, we can derive problem (2.5) by adding (3.1) and (3.2).

\[ \begin{align} \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta + \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta &= \cos m \theta \cos n \theta - \sin m \theta \sin n \theta + \cos m \theta \cos n \theta + \sin m \theta \sin n \theta \\ &= \cos m \theta \cos n \theta + \cos m \theta \cos n \theta \cancelto{0}{- \sin m \theta \sin n \theta + \sin m \theta \sin n \theta} \\ \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta + \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta &= 2 \cos m \theta \cos n \theta \\ \cos m \theta \cos n \theta &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \cos \left( m + n \right) \theta + \cos \left( m - n \right) \theta \right] \end{align} \]
